History

One Piece is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Eiichiro Oda. It has been serialized in Shueisha's shōnen manga magazine Weekly Shōnen Jump since July 1997, with its individual chapters compiled in 108 tankōbon volumes as of March 2024. The story follows the adventures of Monkey D. Luffy and his crew, the Straw Hat Pirates, where he explores the Grand Line in search of the mythical treasure known as the "One Piece" in order to become the next King of the Pirates. The manga spawned a media franchise, having been adapted into a festival film by Production I.G, and an anime series by Toei Animation, which began broadcasting in 1999. Additionally, Toei has developed fourteen animated feature films, one original video animation, and thirteen television specials. Several companies have developed various types of merchandising and media, such as a trading card game and numerous video games.
The manga series was licensed for an English language release in North America and the United Kingdom by Viz Media and in Australia by Madman Entertainment. The anime series was licensed by 4Kids Entertainment for an English-language release in North America in 2004 before the license was dropped and subsequently acquired by Funimation in 2007. One Piece has received praise for its storytelling, world-building, art, characterization, and humor. It has received many awards and is ranked by critics, reviewers, and readers as one of the best manga of all time. By December 2023, it had over 523.2 million copies in circulation in 61 countries and regions worldwide, making it the best-selling manga series in history, and the best-selling comic series printed in book volume. Several volumes of the manga have broken publishing records, including the highest initial print run of any book in Japan. In 2015 and 2022, One Piece set the Guinness World Record for "the most copies published for the same comic book series by a single author". It was the best-selling manga for eleven consecutive years from 2008 to 2018, and is the only manga that had an initial print of volumes of above 3 million continuously for more than 10 years, as well as the only that had achieved more than 1 million copies sold in all of its over 100 published tankōbon volumes. One Piece is the only manga whose volumes have ranked first every year in Oricon's weekly comic chart existence since 2008.

Storyline

"The Beginning": The story kicks off with Monkey D. Luffy, a young and ambitious boy, setting out to sea in search of the legendary treasure left behind by the Pirate King, Gol D. Roger. Luffy dreams of becoming the Pirate King himself. Along the way, he consumes a Devil Fruit, gaining the power to stretch his body like rubber but losing the ability to swim. "Forming the Crew": Luffy gradually gathers his crew, each member possessing unique skills and dreams of their own. This includes Roronoa Zoro, a skilled swordsman; Nami, a talented navigator; Usopp, a sharpshooter and storyteller; Sanji, a master chef and expert martial artist; Tony Tony Chopper, a reindeer who can transform into various forms; Nico Robin, an archaeologist with knowledge of the ancient world; Franky, a cyborg shipwright; Brook, a living skeleton musician; and later, Jinbe, a Fishman martial artist and helmsman. "Grand Line Adventures": The crew embarks on a journey through the Grand Line, encountering numerous islands, powerful adversaries, and mythical creatures along the way. They clash with the Marines, other pirate crews, and the enigmatic Seven Warlords of the Sea. Each member of the crew faces personal challenges and grows stronger, both individually and as a team. "Struggles and Triumphs": Throughout their journey, the Straw Hats face formidable foes such as the tyrannical World Government, the notorious pirate crew, the Yonko (Emperors of the Sea), and the secret organization, Cipher Pol. They also confront the mysterious power of the ancient weapons, which have the potential to reshape the world. "The Road to the One Piece": Luffy and his crew uncover clues about the location of the One Piece, hidden on the legendary island of Raftel at the end of the Grand Line. Their journey becomes increasingly perilous as they navigate through dangerous seas and face off against stronger adversaries. Along the way, they forge alliances with other pirate crews and rebel factions, all while remaining true to their ideals of freedom and friendship. "Final Confrontations": As they near the end of their journey, the Straw Hats find themselves in a final showdown against the World Government and other powerful factions vying for control over the One Piece. Luffy's determination and the bonds of friendship among the crew are put to the ultimate test as they fight to realize their dreams and uncover the truth behind the history of the world. "Legacy and Beyond": In the aftermath of their adventures, Luffy and his crew leave a lasting impact on the world, inspiring others to pursue their dreams and challenge the status quo. The mysteries of the One Piece are finally revealed, but the journey doesn't end there, as new adventures and challenges await the Straw Hat Pirates and those who dare to sail the seas.
